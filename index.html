About

MSc Artificial Intelligence student with research experience in neuro-symbolic AI, formal methods, search-based AI and efficient machine learning. My interests lie in combining learning-based models with symbolic representations, executable structures, and algorithmic reasoning methods to build AI systems that are more reliable, interpretable and capable of structured problem solving. I am especially interested in PhD research on neuro-symbolic reasoning, formal methods and AI for search and optimization.

Research Interests

My research interests span neuro-symbolic AI, formal reasoning and learning-based methods. Going forward, I am especially interested in neuro-symbolic approaches that connect these areas to improve the reliability, interpretability and reasoning capabilities of modern AI systems.

  • Neuro-Symbolic AI — Combining learning-based models with symbolic representations such as knowledge graphs, formal languages, executable queries and constraints.
  • Reliable and Formal AI — Applying logic-based methods, formal verification, automated reasoning and theorem proving to study and improve the correctness of intelligent systems.
  • Search, Planning and Optimization — Developing algorithmic and learning-guided approaches for structured search, planning, hypothesis generation and combinatorial reasoning.
